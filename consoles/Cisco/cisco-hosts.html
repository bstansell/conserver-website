This document is meant to show which adapters you would use to connect a variety of devices to Cisco console ports, or to patch panel ports. Information on other brands of console servers, as well as links to logging software and basic serial information can be found on my Greater Scroll of Console Knowledge.

If you use a Lantronix terminal server device, you should be able to use the adapters listed for Cisco Patch Panels. (The Lantronix wiring format ifor most of their products is a mirror of the Cisco Console wiring format.).

If you use a Digi CM series console server, you can use the Cisco adapters for basic data-only connections. My earlier information that the Digi CM was wired identically to Cisco seems to have been wrong. While the data leads will work, the hardware flow control from the attached device to the CM is not the same, and could result in lost data if you need to rely on this hardware signal.

If you connect your SUN computers to a Console Server, you should take a quick read through our serial BREAK testing pages for information you may find useful.

The list below presumes the following:

  1. The adapters are purchased from Cisco(*) or APA Cables & Networks.
  2. The adapters are connected with a straight-through cable, not a 'rolled' cable.

(* Cisco only made the DB-25 adapters, and one DB-9 adapter. APA Cables & Networks sells all of the adapters listed, as well as the specialty cables. See the APA Cables & Networks Cisco Kit page for part numbers and other info.)

    The Cisco 2511-RJ servers provided 8 or 16 RJ-45 ports, wired the same as the Cisco RJ-45 Console and AUX ports. The patch panels from APA Cables & Networks are wired the null-modem opposite from the Cisco 2511-RJ ports. More info is available from the patch panel section of the Cisco Console Guide.

The table below shows various devices, followed by;

  • port is indicates the signalling coming from the device.
  • Console indicates what cable or adapter you would use to connect that device to a Cisco RJ-45 Console Port (or RJ-45 AUX port, or a 2511-RJ access server port.)
  • Patch Panel indicates what cable or adapter to use to connect that device to an APA Cables & Networks patch panel, or to a Cisco CAB-OCTAL-ASYNC RJ-45 hydra cable.
